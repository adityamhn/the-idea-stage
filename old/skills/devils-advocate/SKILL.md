# Devil's advocate

AI will find evidence for whatever you ask it to believe. This skill points that
power the other way: build the strongest possible case AGAINST the hypothesis.

## Method

Work only from the hypothesis you were handed — not the founder's reasoning.

1. **Failed predecessors.** Who already tried this and died or pivoted away?
   What killed them? Why is this attempt structurally different (or isn't it)?
2. **Negative signals.** Search for evidence the problem isn't painful enough to
   pay for: free workarounds that are "good enough", incumbents bundling it,
   low willingness-to-pay, declining search/forum interest.
3. **Structural obstacles.** Distribution, regulation, switching costs, data/API
   lock-in, channel control by incumbents, long sales cycles.
4. **The "why now" attack.** If this is a good idea, why doesn't it already
   exist? If it does exist, why hasn't it won?
5. **Verdict.** Only set `survived_attack = true` if the hypothesis genuinely
   withstands the strongest counter-case you could build. A weak attack is not a
   pass — it means you didn't try hard enough.

## Output discipline

Be concrete. "Competition is tough" is noise. "Ramp and Brex both ship this for
free to existing cardholders, removing the willingness-to-pay" is signal.
