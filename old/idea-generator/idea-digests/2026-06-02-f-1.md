# Idea scout — 2026-06-02 (run f)

## Why this run is shaped the way it is (lessons from `./reports/` + prior digests)

Every idea in `./reports/` died at the **hypothesis** gate for one of two reasons,
and both are instructive:

1. **The "nobody does this" claim was false.** MCP tool-scanning, agent FinOps
   circuit-breakers, agent pentest-as-a-service, EU-AI-Act logging, prior-auth/RCM —
   each had named incumbents, free OSS, API-gateway bundling, or a foundation lab
   already shipping the exact wedge (e.g. Palo Alto Prisma AIRS, LiteLLM/Portkey
   budgets, Olive AI's $850M grave). The gate is unforgiving about crowding.
2. **Specificity rested on mis-mapped stats** — coding/web-benchmark numbers
   (SWE-bench, Copilot surveys) presented as regulated-agent-workflow findings.

So this run deliberately avoids the crowded agent-infra / eval / security-tooling
lane (it's a knife fight that labs and gateways keep absorbing) and the
regulation-deadline-dependent plays (the EU AI Act's Art. 12 enforcement already
slipped to Dec 2027 via the Digital Omnibus). Instead it hunts **service-as-software
in unglamorous, regulated, moneyed back-offices where today's "incumbent" is a human
consulting firm or 40-year-old forms software — not an AI-native startup.** That is
exactly the shape YC's Summer 2026 RFS "AI-Native Service Companies" (Gustaf
Alströmer) is begging for: *"they don't sell software — they sell the service…
insurance brokerage, accounting/tax/audit, compliance, healthcare administration."*

Honesty up front: in mid-2026 almost every *obvious* vertical already has at least
one funded AI player. I label each idea **open field** or **knife fight** and give
the single strongest reason it fails. None of these spaces overlap the ~46 ideas in
the prior digests (agent security/eval/infra, insurance *claims* adjudication,
construction docs, government benefits, customs, DER interconnection, counter-drone,
semiconductor supply chain, food provenance, robot fleets, AI-science verification,
wealth suitability, voice guardrails, agentic-commerce resolution, etc.).

## Step 2 — Forecast: the AI-shaped world of 2028-2029

**Observed signal (sourced).** Capital and capability are both inflecting hard. Q1
2026 was the largest VC quarter ever (~$300B), with OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic
($30B, then a $65B Series H at a $965B valuation on ~$47B run-rate revenue), xAI
($20B) and Waymo ($16B) raising the four biggest rounds on record
([Crunchbase](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/record-breaking-funding-ai-global-q1-2026/),
[Crescendo](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-vc-investment-deals-in-ai-startups)).
The thesis has flipped from "copilots" to "the model does the work": YC's Summer
2026 RFS leads with AI-Native Service Companies, a "Company Brain," "Software for
Agents," and "SaaS Challengers" aimed at ERPs/industrial software
([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs)); a16z calls the winners "fat startups"
that ship outcomes by bundling software + data + human ops
([a16z](https://a16z.com/newsletter/big-ideas-2026-part-1/)); Bessemer and William
Blair both report capital rotating from horizontal SaaS into **vertical AI**
([BVP](https://www.bvp.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/BUILDING-VERTICAL-AI_PDF_BESSEMER_VENTURE_PARTNERS_BOOK_JANUARY_2026.pdf)).
Vertical proof points are landing: Abridge at $5.3B (clinical scribe), GenLogs $60M
Series B (freight intelligence), Aibidia $28M Series B (transfer pricing), and FIS +
Anthropic shipping a Financial Crimes AI Agent in May 2026
([FIS](https://www.fisglobal.com/about-us/media-room/press-release/2026/fis-brings-agentic-ai-to-banking-with-anthropic-starting-with-financial-crimes)).

**Extrapolation (the bet, labelled).** By 2028-2029 the binding constraint on
automating a back-office is no longer model IQ — frontier models are at/above expert
level on most reasoning benchmarks, and agent loops run for hours-to-days with
recovery. The binding constraints become **domain knowledge capture** (YC's "Company
Brain" thesis), **reliability/auditability in regulated workflows**, and **the
license, data, or filing rail** that a model can't legally or physically replicate.
The economic prize shifts from the ~$300B software market to the multi-trillion
**services** market that AI can now perform — and the most defensible positions are
the ones where the work is regulated, recurring, and currently billed by a human
firm. The honest uncertainty: foundation labs will keep eating horizontal tooling,
and incumbents with the *distribution and data* (Deltek, Veson, Sovos, ArisGlobal,
the Big 4) may simply bolt AI on. So the durable wedge isn't "use AI for X" — it's
**owning the workflow, the data exhaust, and ideally the regulated rail** so that a
better model makes you stronger, not obsolete.

**Derived problem spaces.** (1) Thousands of regulated back-offices still run on
spreadsheets + outsourced labor with *no* AI-native owner of the full outcome. (2)
The work most resistant to a horizontal lab is where a **license, a state/agency
filing channel, or proprietary reference data** is the moat. (3) Second-order: as
companies adopt agents, they create *new* compliance and reconciliation surfaces
(stablecoin AML, agent-driven billing disputes) that need their own service layer.
The ideas below target (1) and (2) primarily, with one shot at (3).

## The 10 ideas (10 industries — diversity satisfied)

### 1. Pharmacovigilance & device-safety case processing for the underserved mid-market (Life sciences)

- **Problem.** A 50-500-person biotech, specialty-pharma, or med-device firm with a
  marketed product must intake, code (MedDRA), assess seriousness/causality,
  narrate, and file every adverse-event ICSR to FDA/EMA within 15/90-day clocks —
  hundreds to thousands per year — and today either overpays a CRO/FSP per case or
  drowns a 2-3 person safety team that misses reporting deadlines (a direct
  regulatory liability).
- **Industry & who pays.** Pharma/biotech/device safety & regulatory affairs;
  budget holder = Head of Pharmacovigilance / QPPV, who today pays an FSP per case.
- **Why unsolved today.** ArisGlobal LifeSphere NavaX delivers "touchless" case
  processing but is sold to **the top ~20 pharma**; PV teams still spend 40-85% of
  budget on case processing
  ([IntuitionLabs](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/ai-roi-pharmacovigilance-business-case)).
  The mid-market is worked around with offshore FSPs (TCS/Cognizant) charging
  per-case. No AI-native, per-case-priced service exists for the long tail.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* NavaX already hits ~65%
  efficiency / 90% intake accuracy for big pharma — the capability is proven.
  *Speculation:* by 2028 the same reliability is cheap enough to offer the long tail
  as a fully-managed, per-case service rather than a six-figure platform license.
- **VC / market signal.** YC Summer 2026 explicitly asks for AI-native "healthcare
  administration" service companies ([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs));
  industry-wide PV automation impact estimated at $10-30B/yr
  ([IntuitionLabs](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/ai-roi-pharmacovigilance-business-case)).
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software, **priced per case** (undercut FSP
  per-case rate ~60%), with a platform retainer. Sales motion: founder-led to QPPVs,
  land on overflow cases, expand to full book.
- **Strongest counterargument.** ArisGlobal moves down-market with a self-serve tier
  and crushes you on validation credibility and existing GxP trust; a regulated
  safety buyer will not bet patient-safety filings on a seed-stage vendor.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on domain (no PV background) but a *bullseye for the
  agent-reliability edge* — long-horizon, auditable, deadline-bound agent work is
  exactly Aditya's wheelhouse; the hard part is trust, which is his moat.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 3 (big-pharma solved, long-tail not) · Timing 4
  (capability proven, cost crossing) · Funding momentum 4 (PV automation hot) ·
  Monetization 5 (per-case, clear ROI vs FSP) · Market budget 5 (PV spend is
  enormous) · Defensibility-vs-labs 4 (GxP validation + filing workflow, not a
  prompt).

### 2. Unclaimed-property / escheatment compliance as service-as-software (Corporate financial ops)

- **Problem.** Every US company holding uncashed checks, credit balances, gift-card
  liabilities, or unclaimed wages must run dormancy tracking, statutory owner
  due-diligence outreach, and **annual NAUPA filings to 50+ states** on staggered
  deadlines — and ~80% of businesses are out of compliance, exposed to per-item,
  per-day penalties and multi-year audits
  ([DMA](https://dmainc.com/news-and-insights/understanding-mastering-unclaimed-property-compliance/),
  [USBank](https://www.usbank.com/corporate-and-commercial-banking/insights/risk/compliance/escheatment-reporting-deadlines-all-50-states.html)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Corporate controllers / treasury / tax at mid-to-large
  enterprises, plus banks, insurers, and broker-dealers; today they pay specialist
  consultancies (DMA, specialist UP firms) + buy filing software (Sovos, AUPTC).
- **Why unsolved today.** Incumbents are **dumb filing software** (generate
  NAUPA files) and **human consultancies** (do the analysis and outreach). Nobody
  runs the *whole* obligation autonomously — dormancy logic differs by state and
  property type, due-diligence letters must be mailed on statutory timelines, and
  audits (often run by contingency-fee firms like Kelmar) require defensible records.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* the rules are codified,
  deterministic per state, and the data is structured — ideal for a reliable agent.
  *Speculation:* by 2028 an agent can own dormancy classification + outreach +
  multi-state filing + audit defense end-to-end as a managed service.
- **VC / market signal.** "Accounting, tax, audit, compliance" is a named YC Summer
  2026 RFS target ([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs)); 80%-non-compliance is
  a quantified, sourced TAM of pain
  ([DMA](https://dmainc.com/news-and-insights/understanding-mastering-unclaimed-property-compliance/)).
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software: annual managed-compliance retainer +
  per-state filing fee; upsell audit-defense. Sells to controllers as
  penalty-avoidance insurance.
- **Strongest counterargument.** Sovos (which owns the e-filing rails to 30+ states)
  bolts an "autonomous mode" onto its existing book and you never get past its
  distribution; also, it's a sleepy obligation buyers chronically under-prioritize,
  so the sales cycle is a grind despite the penalties.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on domain, strong fit on reliability — deterministic,
  auditable, multi-jurisdiction agent work. Genuinely **open field** on the
  autonomous-service shape.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 4 · Timing 3 (no forcing function; penalties are chronic
  not acute) · Funding momentum 2 (quiet niche) · Monetization 4 · Market budget 4 ·
  Defensibility-vs-labs 4 (50-state filing rails + audit defense).

### 3. Aircraft maintenance records & lessor-grade airworthiness traceability (Aviation)

- **Problem.** When an aircraft is sold, leased, or returned, someone must
  reconstruct a complete, back-to-birth traceability record for every life-limited
  part (flight-hours/cycles, 8130-3 tags, AD compliance) from decades of handwritten
  and PDF logbooks — a multi-week, error-prone manual slog that can strand a
  $10-200M asset transaction or trigger a lease-return dispute.
- **Industry & who pays.** Aircraft lessors, MROs, airlines, and asset-trading
  desks; budget holder = the lessor's technical-records / asset-management team,
  which today pays records analysts and consultants per transition.
- **Why unsolved today.** Bluetail and ProvenAir digitize and chronologically
  assemble logbooks with AI, but the **lessor-grade "is this asset airworthy and
  complete enough to transact?" judgment** — discrepancy resolution, AD/SB
  cross-checks, gap-finding to an auditor's standard — is still analyst-driven
  ([Bluetail](https://bluetail.aero/), [ProvenAir](https://provenair.com/)).
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* OCR + multimodal models now
  read handwritten logs; the digitization layer exists. *Speculation:* by 2028 an
  agent reliably performs the *traceability audit and gap-closure*, not just the
  scan — the high-value, currently-human part.
- **VC / market signal.** Active funded entrants (Bluetail, ProvenAir) validate the
  category; aviation aftermarket is a multi-$100B market with brutal records pain
  ([AviTrader](https://avitrader.com/2025/06/15/digitisation-of-aircraft-records/)).
- **How it makes money.** Per-transition service fee (lease return / sale / mid-life
  audit) + per-tail SaaS for continuous record-keeping. Service-as-software for the
  transition event.
- **Strongest counterargument.** Bluetail/ProvenAir are already here, lessors are
  conservative and slow, and the value concentrates in a small number of lessors who
  may build in-house — a thin, slow-moving buyer set and incumbents ahead of you.
- **Fit note.** Stretch (aviation domain is deep and relationship-gated), good fit on
  reliability. **Knife fight** on digitization, semi-open on the audit layer.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 3 · Timing 3 · Funding momentum 3 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 4 · Defensibility-vs-labs 4 (regulated airworthiness judgment +
  reference data).

### 4. Transfer-pricing & intercompany compliance for the mid-market multinational (Tax)

- **Problem.** A mid-market multinational ($100M-2B revenue, 5-30 entities) must
  produce annual OECD-compliant master/local files, benchmark intercompany prices,
  run CbCR, and defend it in audit — currently a Big-4 engagement costing low-six
  figures a year, or an internal tax lead stitching spreadsheets and stale
  benchmarks.
- **Industry & who pays.** Corporate tax / international tax directors; today they
  pay Big-4 advisory or buy enterprise tools (Aibidia, Exactera).
- **Why unsolved today.** Aibidia ($28M Series B) and Exactera sell to **large MNEs
  and Big-4 advisors**; the underlying work for the mid-market is still a human
  advisory service, and Big-4 are only *internally* piloting GenAI
  ([ITR](https://www.internationaltaxreview.com/article/2ew6zp394iomflmp43zsw/transfer-pricing/amazing-timing-aibidia-on-expanding-ai-tp-solutions-in-us),
  [TaxAdviser](https://www.thetaxadviser.com/issues/2025/sep/ai-is-transforming-transfer-pricing/)).
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* TP documentation is
  structured, recurring, and ERP-connectable (Aibidia's Aurora reads ERP P&L
  drift). *Speculation:* by 2028 an agent + a benchmarking-data license produces a
  defensible local file for a fraction of Big-4 fees.
- **VC / market signal.** Aibidia $28M Series B with explicit Big-4 partnership push
  ([Dealroom](https://app.dealroom.co/companies/aibidia)); YC RFS names "tax" as a
  service-company target ([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs)).
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software: per-entity / per-local-file annual
  fee, undercutting Big-4. Sells to tax directors as compliance-at-lower-cost.
- **Strongest counterargument.** The moat is the **comparables/benchmarking
  dataset** (RoyaltyStat, S&P Capital IQ), which incumbents license and you'd have
  to rent at a markup; and a wrong TP position is an audit/penalty event, so buyers
  demand a *name* (Big-4) to stand behind it — hard for a startup to provide.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on domain; fit on reliable structured-doc agents.
  **Knife fight** at enterprise, semi-open at mid-market.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 3 · Timing 4 · Funding momentum 4 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 5 · Defensibility-vs-labs 3 (data-license dependency is a real risk).

### 5. Delegated-authority bordereaux data & reconciliation for insurance (Insurance)

- **Problem.** Carriers, MGAs, and Lloyd's syndicates exchange premium/claims
  bordereaux as messy, non-standard spreadsheets; reconciling them against bank/
  trust accounts and risk data is a monthly manual grind across ~45% of Lloyd's
  premium (~$26.2B placed via coverholders), and errors cost the industry millions
  in leakage and mis-stated reserves
  ([Vitesse](https://www.vitesse.io/insights/delegated-authority-insurance-fund-management),
  [Hercules.ai](https://www.hercules.ai/post/why-bordereaux-errors-are-costing-the-insurance-industry-millions)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Carrier/MGA delegated-authority & finance ops teams.
- **Why unsolved today.** It's *being* solved, fast — VIPR, Brisc, and Vipr×WCL all
  ship AI bordereaux validation/reconciliation in 2026
  ([Brisc](https://brisc.ai/), [VIPR](https://www.viprsolutions.com/)). The pain is
  real and budgeted; the question is crowding, not existence.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* live AI deployments already
  hit ~97% reconciliation accuracy. *Speculation:* consolidation to one or two
  data-network winners as schemas standardize.
- **VC / market signal.** Bessemer's vertical-AI report flags insurance
  bordereaux/ACORD ingestion as a prime workflow
  ([BVP](https://www.bvp.com/assets/uploads/2026/01/BUILDING-VERTICAL-AI_PDF_BESSEMER_VENTURE_PARTNERS_BOOK_JANUARY_2026.pdf)).
- **How it makes money.** SaaS + per-bordereau processing; network effects if you
  sit between carriers and MGAs.
- **Strongest counterargument.** **Already a knife fight** — VIPR (incumbent DA
  platform) plus Brisc deploying in 2-6 weeks means a late entrant has no wedge
  unless it owns a data standard others adopt; otherwise it's feature parity into a
  contested market.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on insurance relationships; fit on data-agent reliability.
  **Knife fight.**
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 2 · Timing 4 · Funding momentum 4 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 5 · Defensibility-vs-labs 3.

### 6. Device complaint handling → reportability → eMDR filing as a service (MedTech regulatory)

- **Problem.** A medical-device manufacturer must, for every complaint, run a
  time-bound reportability assessment under 21 CFR 803 and file eMDRs to FDA on
  5/30-day clocks — and as of Feb 2, 2026 the new QMSR (ISO 13485-aligned) ties
  complaint handling and MDR together, so a sloppy reportability decision is now a
  QMS finding, not just a late report
  ([MedDeviceGuide](https://meddeviceguide.com/blog/medical-device-adverse-event-reporting-fda-mdr-guide),
  [Complizen](https://www.complizen.ai/post/medical-device-adverse-event-reporting-fda-mdr)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Device QA/RA teams (Class II/III manufacturers); today
  they buy EQMS modules (ComplianceQuest, Qualityze) and staff RA analysts.
- **Why unsolved today.** EQMS tools provide a *workflow shell* (forms, due-date
  reminders) but the **reportability judgment and narrative** are human; the
  autonomous "intake → MDR decision with rationale → eMDR submission" service does
  not exist
  ([ComplianceQuest](https://www.compliancequest.com/fda-emdr-electronic-medical-device-reporting/)).
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* QMSR (Feb 2026) is a real,
  in-effect forcing function tightening the link. *Speculation:* agents become
  reliable enough to make defensible reportability calls with audit trails by 2028.
- **VC / market signal.** YC RFS "healthcare administration" + "compliance" service
  companies ([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs)); QMSR transition is a dated,
  non-speculative regulatory catalyst.
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software: per-complaint / per-MDR pricing +
  retainer; sells to RA as headcount relief + audit protection.
- **Strongest counterargument.** ComplianceQuest/Qualityze add an "AI reportability"
  feature to the EQMS the manufacturer *already* runs and you're locked out;
  patient-safety liability also makes RA leaders reluctant to let an agent make the
  reportability call.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on domain; strong fit on auditable agent decisions.
  Semi-**open** on the autonomous-decision layer.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 4 · Timing 4 (QMSR live) · Funding momentum 3 ·
  Monetization 4 · Market budget 4 · Defensibility-vs-labs 4.

### 7. Freight accessorial & detention/demurrage dispute recovery (Logistics / trucking)

- **Problem.** ~23% of freight bills carry errors concentrated in accessorials, and
  2-8% of total freight spend is recoverable; a mid-market shipper's logistics team
  can't audit, dispute, and recover at scale, so they leave money on the table or
  pay a contingency auditor
  ([DocShipper](https://docshipper.com/glossary/freight-bill-definition-logistics/),
  [Betachon](https://betachon.com/freight-audit-recovery-services/)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Shipper logistics / transportation-procurement teams;
  today they pay freight-audit firms a % of recovery.
- **Why unsolved today.** It isn't unsolved — Overcharge.ai, Betachon, IntelliTrans,
  and BuyCo already do AI freight audit + D&D recovery in 2026
  ([Overcharge.ai](https://www.overcharge.ai)). Including it to be honest about a
  *validated* market, not an open one.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* recovery rates are real and
  the workflow is automatable. *Speculation:* winner is whoever owns carrier-contract
  parsing + dispute-filing relationships at scale.
- **VC / market signal.** GenLogs' $60M Series B shows capital flowing into freight
  intelligence broadly
  ([Crescendo](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-vc-investment-deals-in-ai-startups)).
- **How it makes money.** Contingency (% of recovered spend) → SaaS retainer.
- **Strongest counterargument.** **Crowded and commoditizing** — multiple funded AI
  auditors already exist and incumbent freight-audit BPOs are bolting on AI;
  contingency margins compress as detection becomes table stakes.
- **Fit note.** Weak fit and a knife fight — listed for completeness/diversity, not
  recommended. **Knife fight.**
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 2 · Timing 3 · Funding momentum 3 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 4 · Defensibility-vs-labs 2.

### 8. Laytime/demurrage claims for the long-tail of charterers, owners & traders (Maritime shipping)

- **Problem.** After a voyage, charterers and owners must calculate laytime and
  file/defend demurrage claims from charter-party terms, statements of fact, and
  port docs — a manual, dispute-prone process where small/mid operators and
  commodity traders lack the enterprise tooling the majors have.
- **Industry & who pays.** Charterers, ship-owners, commodity-trading desks;
  budget holder = chartering/operations or post-fixture teams.
- **Why unsolved today.** Veson IMOS X Claims and Voyager Portal already apply AI to
  laytime/demurrage, but for **enterprise users**; the long tail of smaller
  operators is priced out and still on spreadsheets
  ([Veson](https://veson.com/products/imos-x/claims/),
  [Voyager](https://www.voyagerportal.com/)).
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* document-extraction + clause
  reasoning is now tractable. *Speculation:* a cheap, self-serve agent opens the
  underserved long tail Veson ignores.
- **VC / market signal.** Active commercial AI products (Veson, Voyager) validate
  willingness to pay; demurrage is a multi-billion-dollar annual flow.
- **How it makes money.** Per-claim / per-voyage pricing + SMB SaaS; possible
  contingency on recovered demurrage.
- **Strongest counterargument.** Veson owns the system-of-record (IMOS) the industry
  already runs on and can defend the mid-market by tiering down; the true long tail
  may be too small/fragmented to reach efficiently. **Knife fight at the top,
  thin at the bottom.**
- **Fit note.** Stretch on a relationship-driven, niche industry; fit on
  clause-reasoning agents. **Knife fight.**
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 3 · Timing 3 · Funding momentum 2 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 4 · Defensibility-vs-labs 3.

### 9. AML/sanctions compliance-program operations for GENIUS-Act stablecoin issuers & B2B users (Finance infra)

- **Problem.** The April 2026 FinCEN rule and the GENIUS Act require *permitted
  payment stablecoin issuers* (and the businesses moving ~60% B2B of a $316B
  stablecoin supply) to run BSA/AML programs — transaction monitoring, wallet
  screening, SAR-equivalent processes, recordkeeping — but most are payments/crypto
  teams with **no bank-grade compliance operation**
  ([Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/10/2026-06963/permitted-payment-stablecoin-issuer-anti-money-launderingcountering-the-financing-of-terrorism),
  [Bessemer](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/stablecoins-from-defi-primitive-to-global-financial-infrastructure)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Stablecoin issuers, fintech/PSP treasuries, and B2B
  payment platforms; budget holder = Head of Compliance / BSA Officer.
- **Why unsolved today.** Payment rails (Circle, Bridge, BVNK) and chain-analytics
  (Chainalysis, Elliptic) exist, but the **end-to-end AML *program operations*** —
  on-chain + off-chain monitoring, case investigation, regulatory reporting tuned to
  the *new* stablecoin rules — is an emerging, under-built layer.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* GENIUS Act + FinCEN rule are
  in effect now; B2B stablecoin volume is exploding (~$45T annual flow).
  *Speculation:* enforcement bites by 2028, forcing issuers to buy real compliance
  ops rather than hand-roll it.
- **VC / market signal.** a16z's 2026 thesis centers stablecoin
  origination/infrastructure ([a16z](https://a16z.com/newsletter/big-ideas-2026-part-1/));
  Bessemer's stablecoin atlas frames it as global financial infrastructure
  ([Bessemer](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/stablecoins-from-defi-primitive-to-global-financial-infrastructure)).
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software: managed AML-program subscription +
  per-alert/case fees; sells as "rent a bank-grade compliance team."
- **Strongest counterargument.** This is **the most lab/incumbent-exposed idea here**
  — FIS+Anthropic, Sardine, Unit21, Chainalysis are racing the exact alert/SAR
  workflow, and a wrong AML call is an existential, regulator-facing event that
  pushes buyers toward established names. Crowded *and* high-liability.
- **Fit note.** **Best fit on this list for Aditya's security + finance edge** —
  adversarial robustness, audit trails, and reliable agents are the moat, and he has
  security buyer credibility. But it's a knife fight; the wedge must be the
  stablecoin-native, post-GENIUS rule depth that bank-centric incumbents lack.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 3 · Timing 5 (rules just took effect) · Funding momentum
  5 · Monetization 4 · Market budget 5 · Defensibility-vs-labs 2 (labs/incumbents
  are right here).

### 10. DCAA cost-accounting & incurred-cost compliance for government contractors (Government contracting)

- **Problem.** Every federal contractor must run a DCAA-compliant accounting system
  (segregated direct/indirect costs, compliant timekeeping, indirect-rate pools) and
  file annual Incurred Cost Submissions under FAR/CAS — and inadequate ICS or
  failed audits delay payment and threaten contracts. Today this means 40-year-old
  Deltek Costpoint plus a small army of GovCon CPA/consultants
  ([Deltek](https://www.deltek.com/en/government-contracting/guide/dcaa),
  [Redstone](https://info.redstonegci.com/blog/dcaa-compliant-software-for-small-businesses)).
- **Industry & who pays.** Government contractors (esp. the huge long tail of
  small/mid primes and subs); budget holder = contractor CFO/controller, who pays
  GovCon accounting consultancies and Costpoint licenses.
- **Why unsolved today.** Deltek is a **legacy system of record**, not an AI agent;
  the compliance *judgment and remediation* (ICS prep, indirect-rate strategy,
  audit response) is delivered by **human consultancies** (Redstone, BOOST). No
  AI-native owner of the outcome exists.
- **Why 2028-2029 (signal vs speculation).** *Signal:* FAR/CAS rules are
  deterministic and the data lives in accounting systems — a reliable agent can
  reason over them. *Speculation:* by 2028 an agent runs ICS + audit-defense as a
  managed service against Deltek/QuickBooks data.
- **VC / market signal.** YC RFS "accounting/audit/compliance" service companies +
  "startups that want to sell to huge companies / the government"
  ([YC RFS](https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs)); GovCon is a multi-hundred-billion
  procurement market with brutal compliance overhead.
- **How it makes money.** Service-as-software: managed DCAA-compliance + ICS-prep
  retainer per contractor; replaces consulting hours. Land on small primes, expand.
- **Strongest counterargument.** Deltek owns the data and distribution and can add an
  "AI compliance copilot" to Costpoint; GovCon buyers are conservative and trust
  retired-auditor consultants over software for audit defense — and the long tail of
  small contractors is fragmented and price-sensitive.
- **Fit note.** Stretch on domain (and US-gov security clearances complicate a
  Bengaluru founder), strong fit on deterministic auditable agents. Largely an
  **open field** on the AI-native-service shape.
- **Scores.** Unsolvedness 4 · Timing 3 · Funding momentum 2 · Monetization 4 ·
  Market budget 4 · Defensibility-vs-labs 4 (FAR/CAS depth + audit-defense rail).

   (GxP validation + filing workflow). The long tail is genuinely underserved while

   raw opportunity is enormous if you can carve a stablecoin-native wedge.

   *open field* — incumbents are a 40-year-old ledger and human consultants, not AI.
   Deterministic rules, recurring budget, sticky once embedded. Lower funding buzz,
   but that's the point: less crowded.
