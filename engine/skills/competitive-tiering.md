# Competitive tiering

Founders systematically underweight competitors (competitor neglect). Counter it
by mapping every tier and steelmanning the threat.

## Tiers

1. **Direct** — solving the same problem the same way for the same buyer.
2. **Indirect** — solving the same problem a different way (incl. spreadsheets,
   manual process, "do nothing").
3. **Potential acquirers** — incumbents who could build or buy their way in.
4. **Adjacent players** — companies one step away whose roadmap could swallow
   this space.

## Method

- Populate each tier with named, real companies/products where possible.
- For the single **strongest threat**, make the *most compelling* argument for
  why THEY succeed and the founder does not: better distribution, defensible
  data, lower CAC, switching costs, brand. Not the easy-to-dismiss version.
- Ask: which claimed differentiator is actually defensible vs. easily copied?

## Output discipline

The `strongest_threat` field should make the founder uncomfortable. If it reads
like something they can wave away, you haven't found the real threat yet.

**Cite everything.** Every named competitor and the strongest-threat case must carry
a real source (URL + title + short quote) from your web search. A competitor you
can't point to a source for isn't research — it's a guess. Never invent a company
or a URL.
